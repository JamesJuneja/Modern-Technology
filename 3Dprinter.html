3D Printing

What is it?

3D printing, also known as additive manufacturing, is the process of making a physical object from a three-dimensional digital model

How does it work?

3D printing, also known as additive manufacturing, is a process that creates a physical object from a digital design. It works by building up an object layer by layer. The “blueprint” or digital design for the object is usually created in a Computer Aided Design (CAD) file. The 3D printer reads this file, and then deposits layers of material (such as plastic, metal, or ceramic) one on top of the other to create the object. This process allows for complex and intricate designs to be created relatively easily. It’s used in a variety of industries, including manufacturing, medicine, architecture, and even food production.
